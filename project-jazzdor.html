JAZZDOR

GET LOUD: A Jazzdor Microsite & Branding Project

Within 5 weeks, I was responsible for crafting the visual branding and interaction design in a team of 4 for a microsite to represent European cultural festival “Jazzdor”. The project was an attempt to highlight the festival’s expressive jazz scene with a bold art direction to match; we wanted to mirror the unpredictable and energetic nature of the music. 

This project is not affiliated with Jazzdor in any way.

Role:

Lead Visual Designer, Interaction Designer

Tools:

Figma, Photoshop

Duration:

5 Weeks // Spring 2025

The Context

Jazzdor is a brand that showcases the French and European Jazz scene from conventional & established to experimental & contemporary acts to willing newcomers in its flagship Strasbourg festival.

Our Mission:

How might we effectively communicate Jazzdor’s ethos and needs into a platform that celebrates jazz as a collaborative, and evolving art form for diverse audiences to its fullest?

Concept Development:

As the lead visual designer, I drove an intensive two-week period of visual experimentation, iterating rapidly based on team feedback and critique. We explored multiple directions to define a cohesive visual language that could carry through the microsite and supporting assets.

A crucial part of our project was to extrapolate design principles from precedents such as Karel Martens and Ellen Lupton into our own work. Of which we consolidated into posters and other print designs. Finally, our brand identity took shape into something we can transfer onto web.

Final Project

The final overall look results in a spontaneous and erratic-feeling design, echoing the improvised nature of jazz.

Our visual identity combined bold typography with a vibrant color palette. Anderson Grotesk brought structure and edge. High-contrast black and white provided clarity, while purple, pink, and orange accents added rhythm and warmth.

Micro-interactions:

Movement Indicating Interaction

Animated buttons subtly shift and morph on hover, with continuous movement that invites users to interact.

Stop-motion Scroll Animations

Scroll-triggered stop motion on the band page advances a video frame-by-frame, creating a tactile, analog feel tied to user input.

Motion on Hover

Hover-responsive images animate in a jittery, stop-motion style, adding visual texture and reinforcing the crafted tone of the site.

Reflection

Key Takeaways:

Kill your babies. (FIGURATIVELY!!)

As the lead visual designer I’ve had to make many executive decisions throughout the design process. One of the most important things I’ve learned was to not get attached to the first ideas that pop up because often they won’t be the most effective in the long run. Exploration is vital in finding the best possible solution.

Maybe the real UI design were the friends we made along the way?

This project was able to stand out and be as expressive as it is because within our constraints we learned to break out of them in unexpected and creative ways. As much as I led my team, I learned from them too. It made me realize that work is a lot easier when you like who you’re working with. Go figure?

What Next?

One of the main constraints from this project was that all the design and prototyping were done in Figma. If I had to expand on it, I would consider using a more collaborative workflow between different programs to create smoother transitions and interactions. Additionally, the design itself isn’t very responsive or accessible if it were potentially made into a website. Could use a lot of improvement but the experience was worthwhile :)
