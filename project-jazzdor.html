GET LOUD: A Jazzdor Microsite & Branding Project

Within 5 weeks, I was responsible for crafting the visual branding and interaction design in a team of 4 for a microsite to represent European cultural festival “Jazzdor”. The project was an attempt to highlight the festival’s expressive jazz scene with a bold art direction to match; we wanted to mirror the unpredictable and energetic nature of the music. 

This project is not affiliated with Jazzdor in any way.

Role:

Lead Visual Designer, Interaction Designer

Tools:

Figma, Photoshop

Duration:

5 Weeks // Spring 2025

The Context

Jazzdor is a brand that showcases the French and European Jazz scene from conventional & established to experimental & contemporary acts to willing newcomers in its flagship Strasbourg festival.

Our Mission:

How might we effectively communicate Jazzdor’s ethos and needs into a platform that celebrates jazz as a collaborative, and evolving art form for diverse audiences to its fullest?

Concept Development:

As the lead visual designer, I drove an intensive two-week period of visual experimentation, iterating rapidly based on team feedback and critique. We explored multiple directions to define a cohesive visual language that could carry through the microsite and supporting assets.

A crucial part of our project was to extrapolate design principles from precedents such as Karel Martens and Ellen Lupton into our own work. Of which we consolidated into posters and other print designs. Finally, our brand identity took shape into something we can transfer onto web.
