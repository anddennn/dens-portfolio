C.I.V.I.C

“C.I.V.I.C” An award-winning Design Jam Project

Over 10 days, our team developed C.I.V.I.C, a speculative design prototype that imagines a future where an individual’s social value is reduced to a single numerical score. As Lead UX/UI Designer, I was responsible for shaping the user experience and crafting the interface design to reflect the world’s cold, data-driven values which was then prototyped in ‘Protopie’.  

The result was a concept that won the ProtoPie sponsorship award at SparkJam 2025.

Role:

Lead UX/UI, Prototyping

Tools:

Figma, Protopie

Duration:

10 Days // May 2025

The Context:

Set in 2035, C.I.V.I.C is a fictional government app used to verify one’s Social Standing Score, calculated from financial, social, and behavioral data. Citizens use this ID to pass checkpoints, apply for jobs, and even attend social events—illustrating how data-driven systems can reinforce inequality under the guise of objectivity.

The Process:

Concept development:

As a team of 3 we began by brainstorming different directions we could take with this SparkJam’s theme, “Reimagining Experiences Through Time.” There were three different tracks: Past, Present and future. From the first and last track, we explored a few different ideas. None of them stuck because they lacked a ‘stance’.

Eventually, we decided on an idea that would give commentary on one of the most prevalent issues in our modern society, social comparison. Hence, C.I.V.I.C was born.

Our Mission:

How might we leverage speculative interface design as a medium to explore how people are reduced to metrics in digital and institutional systems?

Inspiration:

As the visual designer, I translated speculative themes into a cohesive aesthetic that reflected both contemporary UI patterns and dystopian overtones. For the design I took inspiration from various sources.

Social Media: The numbers matter. Does your profile truly reflect who you are? To an onlooker, it can be the only chance you get at an impression.

Credit Scores: An opaque figure that dictates your abilities related to credit, and other related financial actions.

Black Mirror (TV): As an anthology series, Black Mirror explores our relationship with technology in a dystopian context; a cautionary tale as to what might be in the future.

Final Project

To bring the world of C.I.V.I.C to life, I crafted a visual and interaction system rooted in cold functionality and dystopian aesthetics—where design reinforces the illusion of objectivity and control.

The interface leaned into a clinical, hyper-modern look—evoking the sterility of government systems with a subtle sense of unease. This is done through a monotone color palette and custom-made icons.

Key Features & UI Elements:

Social Standing Score

A dominant score reflects the user’s societal “worth,” with the background color shifting based on status—red for low, yellow for neutral, and blue for high. This ambient cue reinforces hierarchy and subtly shapes user perception.

Skeuomorphic ID Card

A digital ID with realistic textures and lighting to evoke official credentials. The card subtly tilts with phone movement, enhancing tactility and reinforcing its perceived authority.

Score History Visualization

Users can view the fluctuations in their score over time via stark, linear graphs. They can track the changes and exact numbers through a slider.

Reflection

From judges that work in EA, Samsung, Microsoft, etc.

Key Takeaways:

You can't solve something if you don't know what to solve

With so much room for creative freedom our team had a hard time figuring out what idea we really wanted to focus on. As a designer, I tend to get ahead of myself and look to visuals right away. But this project helped me realize that you need a base foundation to work upon before you can really start designing.

Eat critique, drink critique, breathe critique

In this design sprint we had to go to multiple office hours and we had to ask for many many opinions in order to make sure we were on track. Additionally, we had to keep each other in check as a team of 3 as well. But at the end of the day, it was all the input we got that made our project the best it can be.

What Next?

This project was made in the span of 10 days, with 2 and a half people on our team so as of now it does lack a good chunk of its features. But if I had the capacity to revisit C.I.V.I.C, I’d try to flesh out the app and all of its pages just a bit more. Additionally, one of our original concepts for this app was to include fake advertisements to go in a more ‘consumerist dystopia’ direction. I would also have loved to make more artifacts to make this world more believable, because who knows how close it actually is?

You can always take another look...
